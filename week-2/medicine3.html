Amoxicillin

Amoxicillin is an antibiotic that belongs to a group of drugs called penicillins. It is used to treat various types of bacterial infections, such as tonsillitis, bronchitis, pneumonia, gonorrhea, and infections of the ear, nose, throat, skin, or urinary tract. Amoxicillin works by stopping the growth of bacteria.

It is important to take this antibiotic exactly as prescribed and to complete the full course of treatment to avoid antibiotic resistance. Do not share your medication with others, even if they have the same symptoms. Inform your doctor if you experience any side effects or allergic reactions.
